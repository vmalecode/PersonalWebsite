Learning OOD While Coding Chess
February 20, 2021

While learning to write SOLID code, a number of symptoms have presented themselves that help me realize when refactoring is required: A function call returning information that is not needed, copying and pasting sections of similar code, or inheritance that doesnâ€™t make sense. While programming chess, it was clear that there were several scanning methods that were required to look across the chess board, and saw this as a good opportunity to share common methods across chess piece objects.
An area where I struggled, however, is in pawn movement; The asymmetric nature of pawn attack and movement is due to the fact that one color moves up the board, and another down. While I could have applied some sort of rotation vector, or control logic to modify a common method that could be applied to both black and white pieces, I opted to write separate methods for black and white pawns. However, if I revisit chess someday, I will use lessons learned to write more generic code.
